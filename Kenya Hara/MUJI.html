muji



In 2001, Kenya Hara took over the MUJI design department. He abandoned the over-packaging design style and advocated a simple lifestyle concept. The poster above is a collection of posters that fully disregards all unnecessary and strives for the highest level of simplicity. The entire image was captured spontaneously. In fact, this type of design is not devoid of design, but it does away with the unnecessary and exaggeration in favor of a condition closer to nature. From 1983 until 2003, MUJI has existed for 20 years. A series of Horizon posters that were published in 2003 not only promoted a new propaganda image but also introduced other customers to the MUJI design philosophy. Publicity activities basically adhere to this concept : the horizon is infinite, it is the widest line that people’s eyes can see, which represents the accommodation of all things in the world. Our eyesight becomes unobstructed as a result, allowing us to comprehend that everything in the world is visible to our eyes. Due to the boundaries of sea and sky are not obvious enough, the design team did not choose it as the major of the poster design, but chose the green grassland and white salt lake with obvious boundaries as the major of the shooting, which is consistent with MUJI’s “no brand” image idea.
