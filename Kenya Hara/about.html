Kenya Hara, a prominent Japanese Graphic designer who was born in 1958. After obtaining his design education from the Musashino Art University in 1983, he joined the NDC (Nippon Design Centre) right away. In 1992, he established the Hala Design Institute, which works in many areas of design, including branding, exhibition design, sign system, packaging, products and books. His aesthetic the essence through the essential: The profound meaning of each reality through symbolic visual solutions that have their origin in Japanese traditional culture.

Ikko Tanaka, an expert of Japanese graphic design who served as Muji's previous art director, named him as the retailer's new art director in 2001. Since then, he has created communication design while also assisting the business in creating a comprehensive vision. He chooses the artwork for Muji's magazine, packaging, exhibits, applications, and advertising based on the original concept that "sometimes simplicity surpasses splendor." Muji has developed into a global icon of aesthetic and intellectual excellence under his art direction.



Throughout his career, he also created a great deal of successful exhibitions that aimed to broaden people’s vision by proposing experimental objects that could stimulate the senses. Re-Design in 2000, Haptic in 2004, Senseware in 2007-09, Architecture for Dogs in 2012, House of Vision in 2013, and Subtle in 2014. In Kenya Hara's view, inspiration comes from life, ignored by others in daily life might sometimes be an excellent design idea. In fact, in this way we can develop habits by observing people or any small little things in our life, hence we can discover more design inspiration from life, so as to cultivate designers’ more acute design thinking. Besides, design is not only a designer’s self expression, it is to find out the problem and try to solve it in the process.


Kenya Hara's Art Style


The inspiration for Kenya Hara’s design idea originates from his daily life in order to reduce the amount of unnecessary components and discover the meaning of design. He is skilled at using white, and by employing a lot of empty space. Hence, the image is clear and concise, evoking feelings of serenity and beauty in viewers. When used with various texture expressions, white will communicate various messages.
